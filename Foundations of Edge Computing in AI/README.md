# Foundations of Edge Computing in AI – Assignment Summary

## 1. Problem Overview
This assignment explored the fundamentals of **Edge Computing** and how AI models operate in distributed, resource‑constrained environments. The goal was to understand:

- How computation shifts from cloud to edge devices  
- The trade‑offs between latency, bandwidth, and compute power  
- How edge architectures support real‑time AI workloads  
- Practical workflows for deploying and testing edge‑based systems  

The problem centered on learning the **core principles** that make modern AI‑enabled IoT systems efficient, scalable, and responsive.

---

## 2. Approach & Methods
To address these learning objectives, I completed a series of structured labs and exercises that covered:

### Edge Architecture Exploration
- Compared cloud, fog, and edge computing models  
- Analyzed where AI inference should run based on constraints  
- Studied real‑world edge deployment patterns  

### Hands‑On System Configuration
- Worked with Linux‑based environments  
- Practiced containerization concepts (Docker fundamentals)  
- Explored networking, device communication, and resource limits  

### AI at the Edge Concepts
- Examined lightweight ML models suitable for edge devices  
- Studied inference pipelines and optimization techniques  
- Reviewed examples of edge‑AI use cases (vision, sensors, automation)  

### Documentation & Reflection
- Summarized findings from each lab  
- Connected theoretical concepts to practical IIoT scenarios  

---

## 3. Results & Interpretation
By the end of the assignment, I gained a strong foundational understanding of:

- Why edge computing matters for latency‑sensitive AI applications  
- How to design systems that balance cloud and edge workloads  
- What constraints (compute, memory, power, connectivity) shape edge deployments  
- How AI models must be adapted for real‑time, on‑device inference  

What this means: 
These labs established the groundwork for more advanced projects in the course — including telemetry pipelines, ThingsBoard dashboards, and edge‑to‑cloud integrations. The concepts learned here directly support building efficient, secure, and scalable AI‑driven IIoT systems.
